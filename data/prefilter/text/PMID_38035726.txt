              2    Supplementary Methods

              3    Clinical information and samples collection

              4         Between September 2020 and December 2021, 56 patients with newly diagnosed locally
              5    advanced rectal cancer received neoadjuvant chemoradiotherapy (nCRT) before surgery at
              6    Fujian Cancer Hospital. The inclusion criteria were 1) rectal adenocarcinoma confirmed by
              7    biopsy; 2) clinical T3-4Nany or TanyN+ without distant metastasis; 3) received long-course
              8    nCRT comprising 50 Gy in 25 fractions and concurrent oral capecitabine chemotherapy; 4)
              9    received surgery within 6~8 weeks after nCRT; 5) Fujian Province resident for >10 years.
             10    Patients were excluded if they met one of the following criteria: 1) patients with metabolic
             11    diseases; 2) incomplete clinical information; 3) treatment interruption before surgery; 4)
             12    patients who received antibiotics within 3 weeks before treatment, or proton pump inhibitors
             13    (PPIs) or steroids within 6 months before or during nCRT, and 5) patients received
             14    immunotherapy and targeted therapy during the perioperative period. The tumor node
             15    metastasis (TNM) stage of each patient and the tumor regression grade (TRG) were
             16    determined based on the guidelines of the American Joint Committee on Cancer (AJCC)
             17    Cancer Staging Manual and the AJCC TRG system1 2. According to the TRG score, patients
             18    were then divided into two groups: the sensitive group (TRG 0-1) and the resistant group
             19    (TRG 2-3). Fecal and peripheral blood samples were collected from patients on the initial day
             20    of preRT. Samples were then stored at -80°C for subsequent analysis.

             21    Analysis of 16s rRNA sequencing

             22         The HiPure DNA Kits (Magen, Guangzhou, China) were used to extract microbial DNA
             23    from     26   fecal     samples       according        to     the     manufacturer's         protocols.        Primers
             24    341F'-CCTACGGGGCWGCAG and 806R'-GGACTACHVGGGTATCTAAT were used to
             25    amplify the V3-V4 region of the 16S rRNA gene by PCR3. Pooled equimolar amplicons were
             26    subjected to paired-end sequencing (PE250) on an Illumina platform following standard
             27    protocols.

              1         Divisive Amplicon Denoising Algorithm 2 (DADA2) software (version 1.14) was used to
              2    process the sample sequences4. Raw amplicon sequence variants (ASVs) with a minimum
              3    overlap of 12 bp were obtained by merging the reads. The representative ASVs were then
              4    taxonomically classified with the naive Bayesian model implemented within the ribosomal
              5    database project (RDP) classifier (version 2.2) based on the SILVA database (version 138.1).
              6    Simpson index was calculated in QIIME2 (version 2021.11) for alpha analysis3. NMDS was
              7    performed for multivariate analysis with the Vegan package in R (version 2.5.3). Biomarker
              8    features distinguishing the sensitivity from the resistant group were identified using LEfSe
              9    software (version 1.0) with a linear discriminant analysis (LDA) score >2 and P<0.05 3.
             10    Prediction of functional profiles by KEGG pathway analysis was performed using PICRUSt2
             11    (version 2.1.0)5.

             12    Analysis of UHPLC-MS/MS metabolites detection

             13         UHPLC-MS/MS analyses were conducted at Gene Denovo Co., Ltd. (Guangzhou, China)
             14    using a Vanquish UHPLC system (Thermo Fisher, Germany) coupled with an Orbitrap Q
             15    ExactiveTM HF-X mass spectrometer (Thermo Fisher, Germany). The samples were injected
             16    into a Hypesil Gold column (100×2.1 mm, 1.9 μm) and subjected to a 17-minute linear
             17    gradient at a flow rate of 0.2 mL/min. Eluent A (0.1% formic acid in water) and eluent B
             18    (methanol) were used for the positive polarity mode. Compound Discoverer 3.1 (CD3.1,
             19    Thermo Fisher) was used to process the raw data files generated by UHPLC-MS/MS for peak
             20    alignment, peak picking, and quantification of each metabolite.

             21          To identify different metabolites between groups, an orthogonal projection to latent
             22    structures-discriminant analysis (OPLS-DA) model was established using the ropls R
             23    package6. Metabolites with VIP scores >1 and P<0.05 were considered significant7. The
             24    validity of the OPLS-DA model was assessed through permutation and cross-validation tests7.
             25    Differential metabolites were further analyzed using Z-scores7, which were based on the
             26    relative content conversion of metabolites.

              6    Confocal laser scanning microscope (CLSM)

              7    Cells were plated in confocal dishes at a concentration of 1 × 105 cells per well. Following
              8    treatment with MG and irradiation, cells were cultured for an additional 2 h and then fixed
              9    with 4% paraformaldehyde. Next, cells were blocked with a 0.1% Triton solution and
             10    incubated with the primary antibody overnight at 4°C. The cells were then incubated with
             11    Alexa Fluor 647-conjugated secondary antibody (Abcam, 1:500) for 2 h. After staining with
             12    DAPI, the cells in the confocal dishes were captured using a confocal laser scanning
             13    microscope.

             14    Flow cytometry (FCM)

             15    The cells were first harvested from either cultures or tissue samples and prepared into a
             16    single-cell suspension using PBS washes and filtration. The cells were then blocked for 15
             17    minutes using 5% Bovine Serum Albumin (BSA) to reduce non-specific binding (cells were
             18    permeabilized post-fixation if intracellular staining was intended such as Foxp3). After that,
             19    cells were typically incubated with antibodies for 20-30 minutes at room temperature,
             20    followed by 2-3 washes with PBS. The following anti-mouse antibodies were utilized:
             21    anti-CD3-APC (eBioscience), anti-CD8-PE (eBioscience), and anti-CD69-FITC (BioLegend)
             22    for detecting the effect of CD8+ T cells8; anti-CD4-FITC (eBioscience), anti-CD25-per-Cy5.5
             23    (eBioscience), and anti-Foxp3-PE-Cy7 (eBioscience) for detecting Treg cells; and
             24    anti-CD3-FITC (eBioscience) and anti-NKp46-APC (eBioscience) for detecting NK cells9;
             25    anti-CD11b-FITC (eBioscience), anti-CD11c-APC (eBioscience), anti-CD86-PE/Cyanine7
             26    (BioLegend), and anti-CD80-PE (eBioscience) for detecting mDC cells10. Stained cells were
             27    then run through the flow cytometry apparatus (BD FACS Melody, USA) and data were

              3    ELISA

              4         Cell supernatants and tissue homogenates were collected for evaluation of cytokines
              5    levels including IFN-α, IFN-β, IFN-γ, TNF-α, CXCL10, IL-1β, IL-10, IL-4, IL-6 and TGF-β.
              6    The conjugate reagent was added to the plates incubated with samples and standard solutions
              7    according to the protocol12. Chromogen solutions A and B were then added to the plates
              8    before analysis. After incubation for 15 min, the optical density (OD) was measured at 450
              9    nm using the Spectro Max M5e instrument (Molecular Devices, USA).

             10    Western blot analysis

             11         RIPA solution (Beyotime Biotechnology, China) supplemented with PMSF (MCE,
             12    China), the cocktail of protease inhibitors (MCE, China) and phosphatase inhibitors (MCE,
             13    China) was used to extract proteins from tumor tissues and cells. Proteins were separated by
             14    SDS-PAGE and electrotransferred to nitrocellulose (NC) membranes after quantification
             15    using the BCA kit (TransGen Biotech, China). The NC membranes were then blocked with 5%
             16    bovine serum albumin (BSA) in TBST for 1.5 hours at room temperature and incubated with
             17    primary antibodies overnight at 4 ℃ followed by HRP-conjugated secondary antibodies for 1
             18    hour at room temperature. Membranes were washed 3 times with TBST and developed using
             19    an enhanced chemiluminescence substrate and the signal was detected using ChemiDoc
             20    Touch Imaging System (Bio-Rad, USA). The band intensities were quantified using
             21    ImageLab software. Table S6 lists the specific antibodies used in this study

             24    preserve their structure. Subsequently, they were treated with 0.1% Triton for a further 15
             25    minutes to facilitate antibody uptake. Once prepared, goat serum was used to block the
             26    sections. Subsequently, the sections were exposed to the primary antibody and allowed to
