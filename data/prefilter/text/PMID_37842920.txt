    ABSTRACT                                                                                                                                       ARTICLE HISTORY
    Recently, an intestinal dysbiotic microbiota with enrichment in oral cavity bacteria has been                                                  Received 27 July 2023
    described in colorectal cancer (CRC) patients. Here, we characterize and investigate one of these                                              Revised 18 September 2023
    oral pathobionts, the Gram-positive anaerobic coccus Parvimonas micra. We identified two phylo­                                                Accepted 20 September 2023
    types (A and B) exhibiting different phenotypes and adhesion capabilities. We observed a strong                                                KEYWORDS
    association of phylotype A with CRC, with its higher abundance in feces and in tumoral tissue                                                  Colorectal cancer; colonic
    compared with the normal homologous colonic mucosa, which was associated with a distinct                                                       epithelial primary cells;
    methylation status of patients. By developing an in vitro hypoxic co-culture system of human                                                   pathobionts; Parvimonas
    primary colonic cells with anaerobic bacteria, we show that P. micra phylotype A alters the DNA                                                micra; DNA methylation
    methylation profile promoters of key tumor-suppressor genes, oncogenes, and genes involved in
    epithelial–mesenchymal transition. In colonic mucosa of CRC patients carrying P. micra phylotype
    A, we found similar DNA methylation alterations, together with significant enrichment of differ­
    entially expressed genes in pathways involved in inflammation, cell adhesion, and regulation of
    actin cytoskeleton, providing evidence of P. micra’s possible role in the carcinogenic process.
