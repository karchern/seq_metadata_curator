Keywords:                                                Peptostreptococcus anaerobius is an anaerobic bacterium, which has been found selectively en-riched in the fecal
Colorectal cancer                                        and mucosal microbiota of colorectal cancer (CRC) patients. Emerging evidence suggest P. anaerobius may
Peptostreptococcus anaerobius                            contribute to the development of CRC in human. In this study, we designed a multi-epitope chimeric vaccine
PCWBR2
                                                         against P. anaerobius PCWBR2, a recently identified adhesin that interacts directly with colon cell lines by
Immunoformatics
Multi-epitope
                                                         binding α2/β1 integrin frequently overexpressed in human CRC tumors and cell lines. Immunoinformatics tools
                                                         predicted six cytotoxic T lymphocyte epitopes, five helper T lymphocyte epitopes, and six linear B lymphocyte
                                                         epitopes. The predicted epitopes were joined with AAY or GPGPG linkers and a previously reported TLR4 agonist
                                                         was added to the vaccine construct’s N terminal as an adjuvant using EAAAK linkers and the order of epitopes

                                                         characteristics. Our study provided a promising design for vaccines against P. anaerobius.
