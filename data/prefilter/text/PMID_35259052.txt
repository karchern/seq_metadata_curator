    ABSTRACT                                                                                                                                       ARTICLE HISTORY
    The gut microbiota was emerging as critical regulatory elements in shaping the outcome of cancer                                               Received 25 December 2021
    immunotherapy. However, the underlying mechanisms by which the gut commensal species                                                           Revised 2 February 2022
    enhance antitumor immunity remain largely unexplored. Here, we show that the gut microbiota                                                    Accepted 9 February 2022
    from healthy individuals conferred considerable sensitivity to anti-PD-1 in the colorectal cancer                                              KEYWORDS
    (CRC) tumor-bearing mice, whereas gut microbiota from CRC patients failed to do so. By 16S rRNA                                                Colorectal cancer;
    gene sequencing, we identified Lactobacillus that was significantly increased in the mice with good                                            immunotherapy; anti-PD-1;
    response to anti-PD-1, and significantly correlated with anti-tumor immunity. After a series of                                                gut microbiota; probiotics;
    screening, we isolated a novel Lacticaseibacillus strain, named L. paracasei sh2020. L. paracasei                                              tumor microenvironment
    sh2020 showed the most notable anti-tumor immunity in the mice with gut dysbiosis.
    Mechanistically, the antitumor immune response elicited by L. paracasei sh2020 was dependent
    on CD8+ T cell. In vitro and in vivo studies revealed that L. paracasei sh2020 stimulation triggered the
    upregulated expression of CXCL10 in the tumors and subsequently enhanced CD8+ T cell recruit­
    ment. Meanwhile, the modulation of gut microbiota caused by L. paracasei sh2020 enhanced its
    antitumor effect and gut barrier function. Overall, our study offered novel insights into the
    mechanism by which gut microbiota shaped the outcome of cancer immunotherapy and, more
    importantly, the novel strain L. paracasei sh2020 might serve as an easy and effective way to
    promote anti-PD-1 effect in clinical practice.
